I discovered design at twelve and found my passion in creating.

I discovered design at twelve, driven by a simple need: I wanted my small YouTube gaming channel to stand out. I realized that a strong visual identity — starting with a custom profile picture and banner — was the key to capturing attention. I didn't just want to participate; I wanted to be remembered.

Sticked with graphic design. Wanted to create more. Posters. Logos. Banners. I fell in love with the process and the ability to play with my creativity.

I spent years doing that for startups, building systems that turned vague ideas into something people recognize and trust. But over time I found myself caring less about how a brand looks and more about what a product does. I wanted to be closer to the problem itself — not just shaping perception, but shaping the thing people actually use. That's what pulled me into product design, and that's where I am now.

The products I want to work on are the ones where design isn't decoration — it's the reason the product works at all.
